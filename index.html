Restaurants Closed Early, Re-Opening Unclear

The coronavirus pandemic has had major impacts on labor in the state.
By Theresa Gaffney
Published May 6, 2020 8:00 PM

Almost 1300 businesses in New York state have temporarily closed since the start of 2020, according to data from the state Department of Labor last updated end of day Tuesday. 72,878 workers have been affected so far.

As social distancing measures continue during the coronavirus crisis, businesses across the state are shutting down or pausing operations due to lost revenue. While the peak of the virus came in early April, March 16 was the peak for closing businesses—185 businesses across the state closed that day, and another 112 the next day.

Restaurants have been hit hardest, as almost 500 have closed, affecting almost 22,000 workers. Almost none of the restaurants are unionized, leaving the workers—many of whom are likely undocumented—with few resources to turn to for support during the crisis.

It’s unclear when restaurants and other businesses in New York state will be able to re-open. The hardest hit industry is currently included in phase three of Governor Andrew Cuomo’s four-step plan to re-open businesses in the state.

“We have spoken to every expert on the globe who has been through this before, and we have come up with factual data points to guide us on reopening,” Cuomo said recently in a statement.
